Java Champions are leaders

I met Víctor through collaboration in the latin america Java community some years ago, he has been actively participating as JUG leader and board member in the Guatemalan Java User Group since 2011, creating a thriving community that as far as I know is the oldest, biggest and most active JUG in Central America. If you wanna know where he has been collaborating you could check his nickname on google (tuxtor) https://google.com/search?q=tuxtor.

During this time we've discussed about Java and Open Source in general, discovering that he started to work with Open Source communities more than 10 years ago, creating a strong leadership record. A couple of participations through the years:

  • 2008 - Software Freedom Day at Guatemala https://wiki.ubuntu.com/sfd/Guatemala
  • 2008 - Ubuntu LoCo Team https://wiki.kubuntu.org/Ubucon/Guatemala
  • 2009 - Country leader for the Latin American Festival of Free Softwarehttps://flisol.info/FLISOL2009/Guatemala
  • 2009 - Sun's OSUM member and leader at college http://tuxtor.shekalug.org/%c2%bffelicidad-frustrada/
  • 2012 - Leadership on many free software communities in Guatemala http://www.slgt.org/grupos-de-usuarios.1.html
  • 2015 - Java Magazine tweet about Guatemala Java Users Group annual conferencehttps://twitter.com/oraclejavamag/status/566145327800258561 - Alternative link (page 13) http://www.javamagazine.mozaicreader.com/JanFeb2015/Default/13/0#&pageSet=13&page=0&contentItem=0

Hence, he has been a fundamental partner in the popularization of Java and computer science in his region. Recently he was appointed as vicepresident of the informatics commission on the guatemalan national science system due his involvment in tech communities, being a leader not only in the Java Community but in the entire CS Area:

  • https://twitter.com/tuxtor/status/977195365463216130
  • http://comisiones.concyt.gob.gt/portal/index.php/comisiones/d-informatica/77-jd-informatica
